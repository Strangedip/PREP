## Scope comparison (what changes)

| Dimension | Tech Lead (L5/L6, SDE3) | Staff Engineer (L6/L7) | Principal / Architect (L7+) |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Team scope** | 1–2 squads (5–12 engineers) | 3–8 squads or platform affecting 50+ eng | Org / business unit (100+ eng) |
| **Delivery** | Owns roadmap for team | Owns technical outcomes across teams | Owns multi-year technical bets |
| **Architecture** | Service + team boundaries | Platform, standards, golden paths | Domain architecture, build vs buy |
| **Coding** | 30–50% hands-on | 20–40% — hard problems, prototypes | 10–30% — spikes, reviews |
| **People** | Mentoring, reviews input | Mentoring leads, hiring bar | Hiring bar, exec alignment |
| **On-call** | Team rotation lead | Defines SLOs org-wide | Incident governance |
| **Interview focus** | SD + coding + behavioral | Hard design + cross-team | Vision + arch council — [Principal guides](../Principal/) |

## Component breakdown (what to negotiate)

| Component | Lead | Staff | Negotiation tip |
|-----------|------|-------|-----------------|
| **Base** | 30–45% of total | 25–35% | Harder to change after join |
| **RSU/ESOP** | 40–55% | 45–60% | Ask **refresh** policy at Staff+ |
| **Sign-on** | Common for switchers | Use for year-1 RSU gap | Clawback — read 12–24 mo |
| **Bonus** | 10–20% target | 15–25% | Amazon vs Google structure differs |
| **Level** | More valuable than +5L | Staff title unlocks scope | Down-level = renegotiate in 6 mo |

## When to push for Staff vs stay Tech Lead

| Push for Staff if… | Stay Tech Lead if… |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| You already drive RFCs across 3+ teams | Scope is single product squad |
| You own platform/IDP used org-wide | You prefer 50%+ coding |
| Offers at Staff band (>70L total at product co) | Staff = people manager track conflation |
| [Staff Loop Expectations](Staff_Loop_Expectations.md) checklist passes | Enjoy delivery over politics |

## Negotiation scripts (India context)

**Competing offer** (factual only):
> "I have an offer from [Company] at [X]L total with [Y]% equity. I'm more excited about [this team]'s [product], but need total comp closer to [Z]L to align with market."

**Level upgrade**:
> "Based on my scope leading [platform/order service] across [N] teams and [metric], I'd like the offer evaluated at Staff / L6 rather than Senior Lead."

**ESOP clarity** (startup):
> "What was last round valuation? What's the preferred price for my grant? Is there liquidity timeline?"

**Sign-on to bridge RSU cliff**:
> "Year-1 equity is below my current unvested. Can we bridge with sign-on of [amount] over 12 months, or front-load RSU year 1?"

**Remote / hybrid in writing**:
> "Can hybrid / WFH days be in the offer letter? Festival on-call fairness and commute matter for retention."

**Counter when they say "band max"**:
> "Understood on base. Can we move total via sign-on or year-1 RSU, and confirm [Level code] in the letter?"

**Decline politely (keep door open)**:
> "I'm going to accept another role that matches Staff scope more closely. I have a lot of respect for [team] — happy to reconnect if a [Level] seat opens."

## Red flags in offer letters

| Flag | Risk |
|------|------|
| No level in writing | Down-level on join |
| "Performance-based equity" vague | Zero refresh |
| Clawback > 12 mo on sign-on | Can't leave if toxic |
| Title inflation without scope | Staff title, Lead work |
| No WFH/hybrid in writing | Bangalore traffic tax |
