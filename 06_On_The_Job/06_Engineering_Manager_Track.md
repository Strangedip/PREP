## The fork: Individual Contributor vs Engineering Manager

Around Tech Lead level, many developers face a career choice:

| Path | Focus | Success metric |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **IC (Staff, Principal)** | Technical depth, architecture, cross-team influence | Systems scaled, standards adopted, technical risk reduced |
| **EM (Engineering Manager)** | People, delivery, hiring, career growth | Team output, retention, psychological safety |

**Neither is "better"** — they require different strengths. This repo's main ladder is IC-focused; this guide covers the EM path honestly.

---

## What changes when you become an EM

| As Tech Lead IC | As Engineering Manager |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| You code 30–50% of time | You code 0–10% (intentionally) |
| Technical decisions primary | People and delivery primary |
| Influence via expertise | Influence via trust and coaching |
| Success = your designs | Success = your team's growth |
| Reviews focus on architecture | Reviews focus on 1:1s, perf, hiring |

**Hard truth**: If you become EM to escape coding without liking people management, you will be miserable.

## EM core responsibilities

### 1. One-on-ones (weekly, 30–60 min)

**Not status updates** — those belong in standups.

| Topic | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| How are you doing? | Wellbeing, blockers |
| Career goals | Growth, promotion path |
| Feedback (both directions) | Continuous improvement |
| Trust building | Safe space for concerns |

**Template questions**:
- What's energizing you this week?
- What's frustrating you?
- What do you need from me?
- Is there anything we haven't discussed in a while?

### 2. Delivery and planning

- Break down epics with team in refinement
- Shield team from randomization
- Communicate risks to product/stakeholders early
- Balance tech debt vs features with Tech Lead

### 3. Hiring

| Stage | EM role |
|-------|---------|
| Job description | Define level and skills |
| Screen | Culture and motivation |
| Loop debrief | Calibrate bar; veto bad fits |
| Offer | Comp negotiation within band |

See [Interview Rubric](../00_Interview_Prep/Mock/Interview_Rubric.md) from the other side of the table.

### 4. Performance management

- Set clear expectations at cycle start
- Document feedback continuously (not surprise at review)
- Handle underperformance with plan: expectations, timeline, support
- Advocate for promotions with evidence

Deep dive: [Tech Lead Conflict and Performance](../00_Interview_Prep/Levels/Tech_Lead_Conflict_and_Performance.md)

### 5. Team health

- Retros that produce action items
- Address conflict early
- Celebrate wins
- Watch for burnout (especially on-call rotation fairness)

## IC vs EM — decision framework

Ask yourself:

| Question | IC if… | EM if… |
|----------|--------|--------|
| What energizes you? | Deep technical problems | Growing people |
| Best day last month? | Shipped elegant design | Unblocked junior → they shipped |
| Conflict style? | Prefer RFC debate | Prefer coaching conversation |
| Meeting tolerance? | Tolerate for influence | Energized by 1:1s |
| Legacy you want? | System that scaled | People who became Staff |

**Try before committing**: Many companies offer **EM trial** (6 months acting EM) or **Tech Lead** as stepping stone.

## EM interview preparation (if you switch tracks)

| Round | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| People management | STAR: conflict, underperformance, mentoring |
| Delivery | How you ran sprint, handled slip, prioritized debt |
| Hiring | How you calibrated bar, said no to wrong hire |
| Technical enough | You don't code daily but must credibly review trade-offs |

**Sample STAR**: "Junior engineer missed deadlines 3 sprints. I discovered unclear requirements, not laziness. I paired on ticket breakdown, set weekly check-ins, velocity improved 40% in 6 weeks."

## EM → IC return path

Returning to IC is **valid** — not failure. Reasons:
- Miss hands-on architecture
- Company needs Staff+ IC more than managers
- Org restructure

**How to return**: Staff-level scope project, refresh coding via side contributions, update resume with EM skills (hiring, delivery) as leadership evidence.

## India market context

| Context | Note |
|---------|------|
| **Service companies** | EM track often faster; watch for "people manager" without empowerment |
| **Product startups** | EM may still code early; wearing many hats |
| **GCC / global** | EM needs strong async comms across time zones |
| **Comp bands** | EM and Staff IC often overlap — compare total comp, not title alone |

## How EM and Staff IC work together

Healthy org:

```
        Engineering Manager          Staff Engineer
        ───────────────────          ──────────────
        People, process, delivery    Technical direction, RFCs, standards
        1:1s, hiring, perf           Architecture review, mentoring
        Removes blockers             Defines golden paths

        Both: team success, psychological safety, quality bar
```

**Friction** when roles unclear: EM makes technical decisions without Staff input, or Staff bypasses EM on people issues. Fix with explicit RACI.
